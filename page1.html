TAKE STUNNING PHOTOS

Just follow the steps and try it out!

Shot: Vivo Y55S (raw)
- Lock the exposure and focus on the subject
- Lower the exposure so you will get a black background
- Center your subject

Shot: Vivo Y55S (edited)
- Start your frame at the water because the thing you want to highlight here is the sky
- Add something in the frame (tree)
- Use blue and orange color toning when editing

Shot: Vivo Y55S (edited)
- Fit the subject on the frame
- Choose an angle that suits your eye
- Center your subject
- Zoom it for about 1.4 - 1.5x so you don't have to capture it closer to the subject

Shot: Vivo Y55S (edited)
- Find something that can add an impression on the beauty of the sky
- Match the height of the branch
- Do not tilt your phone upward, always level it the same as the branch

Shot: iPhone 11 Pro Max (raw)
- Level your phone on the subject
- Fit the subject in the frame including the thing that is connected on the subject (billiard cue stick)
- Zoom it for about 1.4 - 1.5x so that it looks like a dslr shot
- Think about the background (don't take it if there's a person behind that will make the picture unsightly)

Shot: Vivo Y55S (edited)
- Level your phone on the subject
- Be creative with the low light in the subject
- Lower the exposure
- Do not take it on the center, always take it on the sideways

Shot: Vivo Y55S
- Lock your exposure and focus on the sky
- Tilt your angle slightly
- Use something that can stabilize your shot and think about zooming it or not (i used the bleacher seat)

Shot: Vivo Y55S (raw)
- Lock your exposure and focus on the sky
- Here you cannot make the best angle so just tilt your phone and make the angle you desire just make sure to start your frame at the bottom of the building
- As much as possible end your frame (right side) with the sky and not with a wall like the picture

Shot: Vivo Y55S (raw)
- Lock your exposure and focus on the sun
- Match the height of the sun in the frame
- I used the window barriers (railings) so it will have a reflection and make the picture better

Shot: Vivo Y55S (edited)
- Lock your exposure and focus on the sky
- Lower the exposure all the way down until the trees make a silhouette effect
- Fit the electric post on the frame (as much as possible do not fit it just right)
- Use the black and white toning to suit the picture as it express sadness or loneliness



Shot: Vivo Y55S (edited)
- Here I matched the height of the bowling pins picture
- Take it sideways to fit the other bowling lines
- Angle the bowlings pins picture (the center) the same height/level

Shot: Vivo Y55S (raw)
- Lock your exposure and lower it all the way down until you get a black background
- Focus it on your subject because its a close up shot
- Cut the main lamp to showcase the other lamps (blurred effect) and to make the close up shot alluring but if you wanted a picture like the first one on this page, take it on the center and exclude the other lamps on the frame
